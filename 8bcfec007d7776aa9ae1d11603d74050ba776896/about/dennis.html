Dennis Goldschmidt

Behavior & Metabolism Lab

Email: dennis.goldschmidt@neuro.fchampalimaud.org

Summary:

The brain is one of the most complex systems in the universe and we have not yet reached a full understanding of its working principles. I apply my background in physics to questions from neuroscience, in order to develop experimentally testable and predictive models. I want to find out about how brains evolved to perform adaptation and canonical computations under physical constraints.

Origin:

Lüneburg, Germany

Education:

  • 2015, MSc in Neural Systems and Computation, University of Zurich / ETH Zurich, Switzerland
  • 2012, BSc in Physics, Georg-August University Göttingen, Germany

Interests:

Computational Neuroscience; Nonlinear Dynamics; Machine Learning; Neurorobotics; Open Source, Science & Education

Projects:

In work:

  • Identifying neural circuits for nutrient exploration and decision-making in Drosophila melanogaster
  • Developing modular computational pipelines for experimentating with the Fly- and OptoPAD system
  • A Python-based simulation framework for modeling and analyzing agent navigation and collective social behavior

Completed:

  • Interactions of optimal balance and recurrent plasticity for compensating neuronal death
  • Developing a software framework for automated & dynamic pulsed optogenetic stimulation protocols for the OptoPAD system
  • Inducing courtship-related chasing in male fruit flies using virtual females and genetic manipulations
  • Object Fixation and Optomotor Responses in Walking Fruit Flies
  • Neural Mechanisms for Adaptive Vector Navigation in Autonomous Legged Robots
