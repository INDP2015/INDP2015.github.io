Mauricio Toro

Learning Lab

Email: mauricio.toro@neuro.fchampalimaud.org

Summary:

I'm a rodent teacher and thought listener at the Learning Lab under the direction of Joe Paton here at the CCU. I'm also the father of Ramona and husband of Amparo, both are joining me in this adventure here in Lisbon.

Origin:

Chile

Education:

Philosophy BA, 2011, Universidad de Chile; Neuroscience MSc, 2014, Universidad de Chile

Interests:

Dimensionality Reduction; Data Sciences; Behavioral Neuroscience; Philosophy of Mind

Projects:

I've been playing with data analysis at the Learning, System Neuroscience and Collective Behavior Labs here at CCU. In my rotations I was looking at analyses that could be more informative of the underlying structure of some nice datasets. Learning to do spike sorting without feature extraction or engineering, relating OFC activity to breathing phase in a odor-dependent task and how different uses of similar words give rise to different embeddings. Now I'm trying to give birth and raise my thesis project, where I'm mainly looking at the output of the basal ganglia and how it's biasing cortical activity towards rewarding behaviors, the plan is to use ephys, opto and data-analysis to explore this question.
